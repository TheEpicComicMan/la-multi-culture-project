Author Bio | Anne Estevis



Anne Estevis is a pioneering author who introduced many to the Mexican-American culture. She was born around 1936, and was raised in Corpus Christi, Texas. Estevis lived her whole life in the heart of Mexican-American culture, spending much of her young adult life in southern New Mexico, and later moving to Edinburg, Texas, where she now resides with her husband. Invested in education and literature from the start, Estevis has many degrees in education, including a masters from Pan American University and a doctorate from Texas Tech. She taught various grade levels and subjects at schools in New Mexico and Texas, and is the author of two realistic-fiction books about Mexican-American culture and communities. Down Garrapata Road is her debut novel, published in 2003. She then went on to write a second book titled Chicken Foot Farm in 2008. Estevis is very invested in Mexican-American culture, an interest that stemmed from her mother. Her mother was a true, native, New Mexican, and through her stories and experiences, Anne was able to learn about Mexican-American culture, including its history and heritage. Anne experienced life firsthand in southern Texas, where Down Garrapata Road is set. Her husband Fransisco is also of hispanic descent, strengthening her connection to Mexican-American culture.
